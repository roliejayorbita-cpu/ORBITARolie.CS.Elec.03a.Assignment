**Genetic Algorithm**
  
  A Genetic Algorithm functions as a computational optimization strategy modeled directly after the biological principles of natural selection. It begins by generating an initial population of potential solutions to a specific problem. The algorithm then evaluates the performance of each individual, selecting the strongest candidates to undergo evolutionary mechanisms, such as combining features from two parents or introducing random structural mutations, to iteratively produce increasingly superior generations of solutions.

**Marcov Decision Process**

  A Markov Decision Process serves as a formal mathematical architecture designed for mapping out sequential decision-making scenarios. It is specifically utilized for dynamic environments where the immediate results of an action are dictated by a mixture of deliberate agent choice and inherent environmental randomness. The primary objective within this framework is to compute an optimal policy that guides an agent to maximize its long-term cumulative rewards across various states.

**Particle Swarm Optimization**

  Particle Swarm Optimization acts as a search heuristic influenced by the collective, synchronized movement patterns observed in nature, such as flocks of birds. The method deploys a group of roaming "particles" to explore a multi-dimensional mathematical space. Every individual particle continuously adjusts its velocity and trajectory by factoring in its own most successful historical location alongside the absolute best location discovered by the collective swarm, ultimately converging on a global optimum.
